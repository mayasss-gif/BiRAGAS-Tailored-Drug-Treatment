THE DIAGNOSIS
— Pharma's most expensive failure —

Pharma's most expensive failure isn't bad chemistry.
It's the wrong target.

About 90% of clinical trials fail. And the great majority of those failures trace back to correlation-led target selection — a passenger gene mistaken for the driver.
The genomics, sequencing, and expression-profiling tools that drive industry workflows are structurally correlation engines.
They cannot, on their own, tell sponsors which gene is genuinely causing disease. The right target gets guessed at, not identified.
